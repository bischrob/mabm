# Archaic Southwest Demography References

Purpose:

- Provide a vetted reference set for demographic assumptions in the U.S. Southwest Archaic context.
- Document what is defensible to model as relative trend versus absolute population.

## Scope Note

- For the Southwest Archaic, absolute headcounts are weakly constrained.
- Best practice is to model relative population dynamics and settlement-density shifts, then compare patterns.

## Vetted Core References

1. Vierra, Bradley J. (ed.). *The Archaic Southwest: Foragers in an Arid Land*. University of Utah Press, 2017 (often reviewed/cited in 2018).
   - Verified as the major regional synthesis across multiple subregions.
   - Strong source for broad-period demographic/settlement trend framing.

2. Berry, Claudia F., and Michael S. Berry. *Chronological and Conceptual Models of the Southwestern Archaic*. 1986.
   - Important correction: this is the commonly cited 1986 Berry & Berry title in later literature.
   - Frequently cited in discussions of Archaic chronology, gaps, and large-scale population interpretation.

3. Huckell, Bruce B. *Of Marshes and Maize: Preceramic Agricultural Settlements in the Cienega Valley, Southeastern Arizona*. University of Arizona Press, 1995.
   - Verified monograph for Late Archaic/Early Agricultural settlement intensification around stable water.

4. Matson, R. G. *The Origins of Southwestern Agriculture*. University of Arizona Press, 1991.
   - Verified foundational synthesis for the transition to agriculture and baseline population distribution interpretation.

## Radiocarbon SPD Method (Population Proxy)

1. Robinson, Erick, et al. (2021). "Dendrochronological dates confirm a Late Prehistoric population decline in the American Southwest derived from radiocarbon dates." *Philosophical Transactions B*.
   - Good Southwest-focused example of radiocarbon-derived demographic proxy workflows.

2. Robinson, Erick, et al. (2019). Spatial SPD analysis methods (Journal of Archaeological Science 101:63-71).
   - Useful for spatially explicit population-trend inference from radiocarbon records.

3. Kelly, Robert L., et al. (recent North American radiocarbon database and synthesis papers).
   - Appropriate references for large-scale SPD practice and caveats.

## Interpretable Regional Trend Summary

1. Early Archaic (roughly 8000-5000 BCE):
   - low-density, mobile forager systems; sparse site visibility.
2. Middle Archaic (roughly 5000-2000 BCE):
   - variable density; potential regional contraction episodes under aridity in some models.
3. Late Archaic (roughly 2000 BCE-500 CE):
   - increasing site frequency/size in several areas with maize adoption and partial sedentism.

## Modeling Guidance for This ABM

1. Use relative trend targets, not fixed headcount targets, for Archaic calibration.
2. Treat aridity episodes as stress tests on mobility and settlement persistence, not automatic total abandonment.
3. Use SPD-informed priors as comparative pattern targets:
   - growth/decline timing,
   - regional lag differences,
   - settlement clustering shifts near reliable water.
4. Keep absolute population targets as soft constraints unless tied to stronger local empirical datasets.
