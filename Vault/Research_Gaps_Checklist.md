# Research Gaps Checklist

Priority gaps not yet fully specified in current research stack:

1. Household economy and storage behavior:
   - Storage technology limits by settlement type.
   - Spoilage rates by food class and season.
   - Seed-reserve fraction priors and seed-consumption fallback behavior under famine.
   - Deficit-sharing norms deferred for later phase.

2. Settlement and relocation decision thresholds:
   - Empirical priors for push-pull weights and trigger thresholds.
   - Minimum persistence duration before abandonment.
   - Distance tolerance under repeated shortfalls and destination-choice bias.
   - Empirical priors for tenure-inequality strength (IDD `alpha`) and institutional mitigation.

3. Conflict mechanism evidence:
   - Empirical priors for onset/target/outcome coefficients.
   - Group-size and defensibility effects on attack probability by ecological zone.
   - Recovery dynamics after conflict losses and retaliation-memory duration.

4. Trade network formation and persistence:
   - Empirical priors for gravity exponents and max viable travel-cost radius.
   - Tie decay/reinforcement rates and debt-break thresholds by social context.
   - Good-specific transport-loss coefficients by terrain/weather class.

5. Water system dynamics:
   - Empirical priors for source-type lag kernels and drought-failure thresholds.
   - Settlement-scale water-labor viability thresholds by haul distance/roughness.
   - Water-quality decay/recovery rates and waterborne transmission multipliers.

6. Firewood/fuel constraints:
   - Empirical priors for seasonal household fuel demand by diet, temperature, and craft intensity.
   - Regeneration/depletion coefficients for high-wood vs low-wood pools and gather-distance response.
   - Substitution efficiency/quality penalties and fuel-linked ceramic production thresholds.

7. Labor calendar and task competition:
   - Empirical priors for seasonal task-hour requirements and overlap severity.
   - Age-specific contribution coefficients by task type (haul, field, processing, craft).
   - Yield elasticity/threshold parameters for under-allocation penalties by task and season.

8. Social structure parameterization:
   - Household size distribution and co-residence norms.
   - Marriage/exogamy patterns affecting mobility and exchange.
   - Leadership coordination effects on collective projects.

9. Measurement and validation protocol:
   - Exact metrics for settlement clustering, inequality, conflict, and persistence.
   - Baseline acceptance bands before adding complexity.
   - Out-of-sample validation scenarios.

10. Uncertainty and sensitivity design:
   - Priors for weakly known parameters.
   - Global sensitivity method choice (Sobol/Morris/LHS).
   - Structural uncertainty tests across alternative rule sets.

11. Data architecture and provenance:
   - Standard schema for imported GIS, climate, demography, and disease profiles.
   - Citation/provenance fields required per dataset.
   - Versioning and reproducibility policy for scenario bundles.

12. Ethics and interpretation controls:
   - Guardrails against deterministic cultural inference.
   - Reporting standards for uncertainty and counterfactual claims.

13. Initialization and boundary conditions:
   - Initial settlement/household/state-stock distributions and spin-up protocol.
   - Edge-policy assumptions for migration/trade/conflict/resource spillover.
   - Exogenous inflow/outflow assumptions at map boundaries.

14. Information and perception limits:
   - What households/settlements can observe each season (yield, water, risk).
   - Information delay/noise and rumor effects on migration/trade/conflict decisions.
   - Cost of information acquisition (travel/network dependence).

15. Archaeological observation model:
   - Mapping latent simulation outputs to observable archaeological proxies.
   - Sampling/taphonomic uncertainty treatment in model-data comparison.
   - Proxy-specific acceptance bands for validation and falsification.
   - Deposition-rate priors linking living trait states to accumulated assemblages.

16. Institutional adaptation over time:
   - Rules for changing coordination capacity, leadership integration, and norm enforcement.
   - Conditions for institutional emergence/decay under scalar stress.
   - Effects on conflict, exchange persistence, and settlement stability.
17. Network observability outputs:
   - Snapshot cadence for active edge-ledger extraction (kin/trade/alliance).
   - Minimum edge attributes required for topology inference vs co-occurrence ambiguity.
   - Validation criteria for network reconstruction from simulated logs.
18. Cultural transmission and material culture evolution:
   - Trait representation priors (granularity and trait family definitions).
   - Transmission-mode coefficients (neutral drift, conformist bias, prestige bias).
   - Validation targets linking simulated trait distributions to archaeological style-distance proxies.
